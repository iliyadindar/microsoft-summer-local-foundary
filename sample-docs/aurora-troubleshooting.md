# Contoso Aurora X1 - Troubleshooting Guide

## No Water Flow

If the pump runs but no water comes out of the group head, the heating system
probably has an air lock. Run the priming cycle: fill the tank, hold the steam
button for five seconds, and wait 90 seconds. If the pump does not run at all,
check that the water tank is seated fully - the tank presses a micro-switch at
the back of the machine, and a tank that is even slightly tilted will not
register.

## Error Codes

The Aurora X1 shows error codes on the small display above the dial.

- **E01** - boiler temperature sensor fault. Power-cycle the machine. If E01
  returns within ten minutes, the thermistor needs replacement; contact
  Contoso support. Do not continue brewing with an E01 error.
- **E02** - pump over-pressure. Usually caused by coffee ground too fine or by
  tamping harder than 20 kg of force. Use a coarser grind and try again.
- **E03** - descale required. The machine tracks water volume and raises E03
  after roughly 200 liters. Brewing still works with E03 showing, but steam
  performance degrades until you descale.
- **E07** - water tank empty or not detected. Refill the tank or reseat it.

## Weak or Sour Espresso

Sour, thin espresso almost always means under-extraction. Check three things
in this order: grind size (finer extracts more), dose (18 grams for the double
basket), and water temperature (the machine must show a solid green ready
light before you brew). Extraction time under 20 seconds confirms the grind is
too coarse.

## Bitter or Burnt Taste

Bitterness means over-extraction or dirty parts. If the extraction time is
over 35 seconds, coarsen the grind. If timing is normal, backflush the group
head with Contoso CleanCaf detergent - old coffee oils are the usual culprit
for a burnt aftertaste.

## Machine Will Not Power On

Check the red master switch on the rear panel above the power cord - it is
easy to bump it to the off position when moving the machine. The Aurora X1
also has a thermal fuse that cuts power permanently if the boiler ever
overheats; a blown thermal fuse requires service and is covered by warranty
during the first two years.
