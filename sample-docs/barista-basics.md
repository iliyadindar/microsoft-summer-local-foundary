# Barista Basics - Contoso Roastery Brewing Guide

## The Espresso Recipe

A recipe has three numbers: dose (grams of dry coffee in), yield (grams of
liquid espresso out), and time. Contoso's house recipe for the Aurora X1 is
18 g in, 36 g out, in 25 to 30 seconds - a 1:2 ratio. Change one variable at
a time: if the shot runs too fast, grind finer; if it runs too slow, grind
coarser. Adjust dose only after grind is dialed in.

## Milk Steaming

Fill the pitcher to the bottom of the spout. Stretch the milk (tip just under
the surface, hissing sound) only for the first three seconds, then submerge
the tip and let the milk roll in a whirlpool until the pitcher is too hot to
hold for more than a second - around 60 to 65 °C. Milk steamed past 70 °C
tastes flat because the sweetness-carrying proteins break down.

## Freshness

Espresso is most forgiving with beans rested 7 to 21 days after roast. Beans
younger than five days release too much CO2 and produce spritzy, uneven
shots; beans older than six weeks taste papery. Store beans in the sealed
one-way-valve bag or an airtight container at room temperature - never in the
fridge, where they absorb food odors.

## Common Ratios for Other Brews

For drip coffee Contoso recommends 60 g of coffee per liter of water. For
French press, use 70 g per liter and a four-minute steep. For cold brew, use
100 g per liter and steep 16 hours in the fridge, then dilute the concentrate
1:1 with water or milk before serving.
