# Contoso Aurora X1 Espresso Machine - Owner's Manual

## Unboxing and First Setup

The Contoso Aurora X1 ships with the machine body, a 2.1-liter removable water
tank, a dual-spout portafilter, two filter baskets (single and double shot), a
tamper, and a stainless-steel milk pitcher. Remove all blue transport tape
before first use and wash the water tank with warm soapy water.

Before brewing for the first time, run a priming cycle: fill the water tank to
the MAX line, power on the machine, and press and hold the steam button for
five seconds until the pump starts. The priming cycle takes about 90 seconds
and flushes air out of the heating system. Skipping the priming cycle is the
most common cause of a first-day "no water flow" complaint.

## Daily Operation

The Aurora X1 heats to brewing temperature in 40 seconds. The ready light
turns solid green when the boiler reaches 93 °C, which is the factory brewing
temperature. For a standard double shot, use 18 grams of ground coffee and aim
for an extraction time of 25 to 30 seconds.

The machine enters standby automatically after 25 minutes of inactivity. You
can change the auto-standby delay to 10, 25, or 60 minutes by holding the
one-cup and two-cup buttons together for three seconds, then pressing the
one-cup button to cycle through the options.

## Water Requirements

Contoso recommends filtered water with hardness between 50 and 120 ppm. Never
use distilled or reverse-osmosis water on its own: the mineral sensor needs
some dissolved minerals to detect the water level, and fully demineralized
water causes erratic tank-level readings. The Aurora X1 is compatible with the
Contoso PureFlow F2 in-tank filter cartridge.

## Steam Wand

The steam wand reaches full pressure about 15 seconds after you press the
steam button. Always purge the wand for two seconds before and after steaming
milk. The wand tip can be unscrewed with the flat end of the supplied cleaning
tool for weekly cleaning.
